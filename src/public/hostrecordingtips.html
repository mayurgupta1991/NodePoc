Video Recording Tips

More and more hosts are remote or ar not always there. Personal videos make a huge difference in guests identify with you. Below are a few tips that would make your recording better.

Introduction

Introduce yourself to your guests so they feel personally welcomed.

  • Sit down and talk directly into the camera.

    Imagine you’re having a friendly conversation with one of your guests. If you’re recording the video yourself, use the front-facing camera, and remember to check the lighting and sound levels. You want your face to be visible and your voice to be clear.

  • Share a few details about yourself and your hobbies.

    What makes you unique? What will help quests identify with you? Some hosts talk about their jobs. Others focus on their passions (skiing, cycling, food, music, etc.). Be genuine and welcoming.

  • Mention why you like hosting.

    What is your favorite part about hosting—meeting new people, sharing local knowledge? Whatever the case, the goal here is to connect with your guests on a personal level and help them get to know you better.

Amenities

Video makes it easy for your guests to understand your property’s special features and rules.

  • If you provide internet access, explain how to get online.

    One of the first things guests want to know when they arrive at a property is, “How do I get Wi-Fi?”

  • Explain your house rules.

    Most guests don’t want to read a document about “rules,” so make sure your video includes all the information you want your guests to know, such as where to put the garbage, which areas are off-limits, etc.

  • Walk around your property as you record.

    Use your Amenities Video to show guests exactly what you’re talking about. As you do, remember to check the lighting and sound levels. Make sure the rooms and appliances are visible and your voice is clear.

  • Use your Amenities Video to reduce the number of service calls you receive.

    What questions do your quests typically have? Answer them in your Amenities Video. When it comes to finicky switches and complicated HVAC systems, a picture definitely can be worth 1,000 words!

Neighborhood

Highlight the history and unique aspects of your neighborhood.

  • Share the history and unique aspects of your neighborhood.

    Your guests want to “live like a local.” What can you tell them about their immediate surroundings, so they feel more at home?

  • Mention local businesses.

    Talk about the grocery stores, pharmacies, dry cleaners, and other services available in your neighborhood.

  • Include information about transportation.

    What do your guests need to know about getting around? Is there a bus stop nearby? Where’s the best place to park a car?

  • Address safety issues.

    Your Neighborhood Video is a great opportunity to discuss any precautions you would suggest.

Recommendations

Guests value recommendations from a trusted source like you, their host.

  • Highlight restaurants and local attractions.

    Be sure to include any “insider information,” such as your favorite menu selections and where to find discount coupons for the museums.

  • Include activities for all ages.

    Some guests may be most interested in the local nightlife; others are looking for kid-friendly attractions. Be sure recommend a variety of activities.

  • Mention other resources.

    Does your town’s website or the visitor’s center down the block have additional information? Point your guests towards places where they can learn even more.
